# Agentic Marketing Landscape — GTM Predictions

Predictions on which marketing/GTM tools survive the agentic shift and what’s coming for distribution. Use for content angles, positioning, and narrative.

Last updated: **February 18, 2026**.

---

## Own Data vs Distribution — What Survives, What Dies

### Survives

- **Own data — CRMs.** Claude can’t store your customer data. CRMs remain core.
- **Lead databases** — Clay, Apollo, RB2B. Claude can’t collect 1B phone numbers and emails. Data platforms stay.
- **Niche tools** — Server-side tracking and similar. Survive.
- **Enterprise complex workflows** — CDPs, customer engagement for huge brands. Safe and will do exceedingly well. Waiting for more players in the space.

### Dies

- **Outreach automation** — Dead soon. You’ll tell Claude “Spam all YC founders offering my SaaS” and it’ll just do it.
- **Outreach infra** — Also dead. Agents will soon buy domains and set up outreach automation on their own.
- **Creatives** — Mostly dead. In a few months Meta and Google will generate them inside ad accounts. Some players who do top-tier large-volume creatives for huge advertisers survive; for small businesses it’ll be inside Google/Meta.

## What’s Coming for Marketing and Distribution

**Context:** AI made building free and distribution harder than ever. Everyone ships now. Good luck getting anyone to notice. Current GTM tech is pre-agentic — it can’t sell to agents. But distribution is where more money, attention, and energy will flow than ever.

**When building is free, distribution becomes everything.**

### Predictions

1. **MCP is the new App Store.** Claude picks a tool via MCP. No marketplace, no comparison — the AI picks one tool and the user never sees alternatives. This is the iPhone App Store in July 2008.

2. **Ads inside LLMs are the next trillion-dollar channel.** Someone will build the ad network that connects thousands of chatbots the way Google connected websites. That company will be generational.

3. **Agentic commerce is coming.** AI agents don’t pay extra for brand. They compare specs, read reviews, pick the best value. Every category races toward commodity pricing.

4. **AI-to-AI sales will break enterprise playbooks.** AI agents read docs, compare specs, analyze pricing. They don’t watch demos or get charmed.

5. **Social: completely botted and hijacked.** The best GTM people work the algorithms and get billions of views for free. It’s a volume game once you figure out how to create decent, engaging content. Very soon — if not already — 98% of TikTok and Instagram will be fake AI content boosted by bots to go viral.

6. **Brand is more important than ever.** Almost impossible to replicate with AI. Last moat for selling to humans: story, trust, taste.

7. **The gap between good and bad marketers will explode.** Anyone can generate AI slop for ~$0. It doesn’t work. Taste, viral instincts, and storytelling are your alpha.

8. **Marketing is still a volume game.** Try every channel, scale what works, do 10,000x of it. The tools die, but the hustle doesn’t. Human hustlers who can test everything and scale it remain essential.
